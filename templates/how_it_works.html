The VWAP Recovery Pattern

science Technical Deep Dive schedule 20 min read

Our trading strategy is built on a single, repeatable pattern that occurs hundreds of times per day across liquid markets. Understanding this pattern is key to understanding how Railyard Markets operates.

What is VWAP?

The Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP) is the average price a stock trades at throughout the day, weighted by volume. Institutional traders use VWAP as a benchmark for execution quality — their goal is to fill large orders close to this average price.

When price deviates from VWAP, institutional algorithms automatically adjust their orders to pull price back toward the average. This creates predictable micro-reversions that our system captures.

The 3-Step Pattern

Our Recovery Engine identifies a specific 3-step price geometry that signals an imminent reversal:

1 Initial Decline

Price drops from a recent high. This decline creates the opportunity — sellers push price below VWAP, triggering institutional rebalancing algorithms.

2 50% Recovery

Price rebounds by 50% of the initial decline. This confirms that buyers are absorbing the selling pressure and institutional orders are activating.

3 50% Retracement

Price pulls back by 50% of the recovery. This final test creates the "last low" — the optimal entry point where selling pressure is exhausted.

Entry Confirmation

After the pattern completes, we wait for confirmation: price must climb a configurable threshold (typically 1.5-2%) from the last low. This climb confirms that the recovery is genuine and not a false signal.

If price fails to climb the threshold and instead falls further, we do not enter — no capital is risked on unconfirmed patterns.

Risk Management

Every trade operates within strict parameters:

  • Position Size: Fixed percentage of deployed capital (typically 10% per stock in an 8-stock portfolio)
  • Stop Loss: Automatic exit at -0.5% from entry price
  • Profit Targets: Two-tier system at +0.75% and +2.0%
  • Daily Loss Limit: All trading halts if daily losses exceed -1.5%
  • Intraday Only: All positions close before market close — zero overnight risk

The X then X+ Exit Strategy

Once entered, our system uses a unique two-target exit approach:

  1. When price reaches Target 1 (+0.75%), we wait
  2. If price continues to Target 2 (+2.0%), we sell at Target 2
  3. If price returns to Target 1, we sell at Target 1
  4. If price hits the stop loss (-0.5%), we exit immediately

This approach maximizes profit on strong moves while protecting gains on weaker recoveries.

Why It Works

The strategy exploits a structural asymmetry in the market:

Large institutional traders must execute gradually to avoid moving the market against themselves. When price drifts too far from VWAP, their algorithms automatically rebalance — buying when price is below VWAP, selling when above.

Our system positions itself on the other side of this rebalancing behavior, capturing the predictable micro-reversions that result from institutional order flow.
